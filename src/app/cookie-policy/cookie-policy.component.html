1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device when you visit websites. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

2. Does WebPizza Use Cookies?

No, WebPizza does NOT use cookies.

We do not use traditional cookies for tracking, analytics, or any other purpose. Our application is designed to function entirely without cookies.

3. Browser Storage Technologies We Use

While we don't use cookies, we do use browser storage technologies to make the application work:

3.1 IndexedDB

  • Purpose: Store document embeddings and chunks locally
  • Data Stored: Your uploaded PDF content, text chunks, and vector embeddings
  • Duration: Until you clear browser data or delete it manually
  • Privacy: All data stays on your device, never sent to servers

3.2 Cache API / Local Storage

  • Purpose: Cache AI models for faster loading
  • Data Stored: Large language models (LLM) and embedding model files
  • Size: Can be several gigabytes
  • Duration: Until you clear browser cache
  • Privacy: Only contains public AI models, no personal data

3.3 Session Storage

  • Purpose: Temporary application state during your session
  • Data Stored: UI state, selected model, current document name
  • Duration: Cleared when you close the browser tab
  • Privacy: Temporary, automatically cleared

4. Third-Party Cookies

We do not intentionally use third-party cookies. However:

  • Vercel (Hosting): May use minimal cookies for infrastructure purposes
  • CDN Providers: May set cookies when downloading AI models

These are outside our control. Please refer to their respective cookie policies:

  • Vercel Cookie Policy

5. How to Manage Browser Storage

You can clear the data WebPizza stores at any time:

Chrome / Edge

  1. Press F12 to open DevTools
  2. Go to "Application" tab
  3. Under "Storage", click "Clear site data"

Firefox

  1. Press F12 to open DevTools
  2. Go to "Storage" tab
  3. Right-click on "IndexedDB" and select "Delete All"

Safari

  1. Go to Safari > Preferences > Privacy
  2. Click "Manage Website Data"
  3. Find this website and click "Remove"
